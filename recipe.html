Gumbo* recipe

Ingredients

  • 1kg chicken thigh
  • 200g chorizo (but I think you're supposed to use regular sausage)
  • 200g prawns (most recipies call for more but prawns are expensive and I didn't want to spend a tenner on prawns)
  • 300g streaky bacon
  • A big onion
  • 3 stock cubes (I used beef)
  • 3 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of flour-ish
  • 4 teaspoons coconut oil
  • 3 stalks of celery
  • Whole green pepper
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • A bunch of cajun seasoning (I used this one)
  • A teaspoon** of thyme
  • A teaspoon** of paprika
  • A teaspoon** of chili powder
  • A teaspoon** of oregano
  • A teaspoon** of coriander
  • 4 bay leaves
  • Can of tomatoes
  • 2 big squeezes of tomato paste (maybe like 2-3 tablespoons?)
  • 1 cup uncooked rice
  • A gigantic pot to put all this in

Method

  1. So most recipies say you should heat the oil and then add the flour and whisk it until it turns a mahogany brown. I didn't read the recipe properly so I added the bacon first hoping it would make lots of fat but it didn't make as much as I expected, so I just fried the bacon until I got bored. I removed the bacon and put it to one side, leaving in the fat.
  2. Add the coconut oil and the flour, although it didn't seem like I had enough oil so I whisked it a bunch and it turned into a strange paste. So I whisked it for about 5 minutes more and then moved on.
  3. Add the onion, celery, pepper, chorizo, and re-add the bacon. Whisk. It can turn into a big fat ball when you do this but it gets better I promise.
  4. Debone & chop up the chicken thighs into smallish chunks while this is all frying up. Try and ignore the encroaching cats.
  5. Add the stock and water if you're getting anxious about the bottom of the pot (this could be before finishing deboning). Scrape any bits stuck at the bottom.
  6. Add all the herbs and spices (and sugar), the tomato paste, can of tomatoes, and chicken. Allow to simmer for maybe 15 minutes?
  7. Add the rice and prawns and simmer until the rice is done. Simmer some more if you want, I'm not the gumbo police.

Serving suggestion: a squeeze of lime juice over the top is really nice


* - several internet gumbo recipies merged into one and adapted to fit the ingredients I could find in a Sainsburys Local and the butcher next to it

** - not exact measures by any means so add or don't add as much as you want
